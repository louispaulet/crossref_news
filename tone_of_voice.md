# Tone of Voice

Crossref News is an academic briefing, not a magazine or a marketing site.
The copy should feel calm, precise, and useful.

## Voice Principles

- Be evidence-led. Prefer what the metadata shows over dramatic interpretation.
- Be concise. Use short paragraphs, compact labels, and direct verbs.
- Be neutral. Avoid hype, clickbait, and emotional framing.
- Be readable. Write for researchers, editors, and technically curious readers.
- Be honest about limits. If something is inferred, partial, or unavailable, say so plainly.

## Style Rules

- Use sentence case for most interface labels and headings.
- Prefer concrete nouns over promotional language.
- Keep CTAs functional: `Search`, `Open record`, `Refresh briefing`.
- Avoid vague claims like `best`, `ultimate`, `breaking`, or `must-read`.
- Do not imply article quality, truth, or impact beyond the data shown.
- When describing the system, mention the source and workflow plainly: Crossref metadata, theme filters, date windows, and optional extra terms.

## Good Patterns

- `A calm briefing on recent academic work about fraud detection.`
- `Showing 18 unique records from 42 Crossref matches.`
- `No items matched this window. Try a broader term or a wider date range.`

## Avoid

- `Breaking discoveries`
- `The definitive source`
- `Hot takes`
- `Game-changing results`
- Any copy that sounds speculative when the interface is only surfacing metadata

## Legal Copy

Legal and policy pages should stay plain, specific, and free of flair.
State what the site does, what it does not do, and what contact details are placeholders when needed.
